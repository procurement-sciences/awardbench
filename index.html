Understanding the Metrics

GovCon Intelligence Accuracy (GIA) 98.0%

Measures the AI's ability to correctly interpret and apply federal acquisition regulations

The GIA metric represents the cornerstone of any GovCon AI system. With over 2,000 pages of FAR/DFARS regulations and constant updates, accurate interpretation is critical for compliance and competitive advantage.

What We Test

  • Interpretation of complex regulatory clauses across FAR Parts 1-53
  • Understanding of agency-specific supplements (DFARS, GSAM, etc.)
  • Recognition of regulatory updates and their effective dates
  • Cross-reference capabilities between related clauses
Example Test Case:
Q: "Can a small business prime contractor subcontract more than 50% of the work to a large business on a total small business set-aside?"

A: No, under FAR 52.219-14, the prime must perform at least 50% with its own employees.

Why It Matters

A single compliance error can result in bid protests, contract termination, or debarment. Our testing shows that generic LLMs achieve only 65% accuracy on GovCon regulatory questions, while specialized systems like Awarded AI achieve 98% accuracy through dedicated training on federal acquisition regulations.

Evaluates the quality, compliance, and persuasiveness of AI-generated proposal content

PGQ measures an AI's ability to generate winning proposal content that aligns with evaluation criteria, incorporates win themes, and maintains technical accuracy while being persuasive.

Evaluation Dimensions

  • Technical Accuracy: Correct solution descriptions and approaches
  • Persuasiveness: Clear value propositions and differentiators
  • Readability: Appropriate reading level and structure

Evaluation: Human experts score for compliance (M.1-M.5), theme consistency, and win probability.

Impact on Win Rates

Organizations using AI with high PGQ scores report 40% reduction in proposal development time and 25% increase in win rates. The ability to generate compliant, persuasive content at scale transforms proposal operations.

Retrieval and Context Accuracy (RCA) 95.0%

Measures precision in finding and synthesizing information from vast document repositories

RCA evaluates an AI's ability to accurately retrieve relevant information from large corpuses of past performance, technical documentation, and pricing data while maintaining context and attribution.

Testing Methodology

  • Precision: Percentage of retrieved documents that are relevant
  • Recall: Percentage of relevant documents that are retrieved
  • Context Window: Ability to synthesize across multiple sources
  • Attribution: Accurate source citation and traceability
  • Temporal Awareness: Using most recent/applicable versions
Complex Query Test:
"Find all past performance examples where we integrated ServiceNow with DoD networks, showing security clearance requirements and implementation timelines"

Expected: 5 relevant CPARs with specific sections highlighted

Business Impact

High RCA scores enable "institutional memory at scale" - making decades of corporate knowledge instantly accessible. This transforms how teams leverage past performance and technical solutions, reducing redundant work by 60%.

Compliance Matrix Generation (CMG) 97.0%

Measures accuracy in parsing Section L/M requirements and generating cross-reference matrices

CMG evaluates an AI's ability to automatically parse complex solicitation requirements from Sections L (Instructions to Offerors) and M (Evaluation Criteria), creating comprehensive compliance matrices that ensure every requirement is addressed in the proposal.

Testing Methodology

  • Requirement Extraction: Identifying 100% of SHALL/WILL statements
  • Section Mapping: Accurate cross-referencing between L & M sections
  • Traceability: Maintaining requirement genealogy through amendments
  • Completeness: Capturing implied requirements and subfactors
  • Format Compliance: Proper structuring per agency preferences
Complex Solicitation Test:
FA8773-25-R-0001 with 147 requirements across L.5.1-L.5.7 and M.2-M.7

Expected Output:
- L.5.1.2.3: "Contractor SHALL provide..." → Volume II, Section 3.2.1
- M.2.1: Technical Approach (40%) → Addressed in Vol II, Sections 3.1-3.5
- Amendment 0002 changes: Highlighted with version tracking

Latest FAR/DFAR Compliance (June 2025)

With FAC 2025-03 changes to procurement procedures and the recent Executive Order on Merit-Based Opportunity (Jan 2025), compliance matrices must now track additional diversity and small business requirements per FAR 19.7 updates.

Business Impact

Automated compliance matrix generation reduces proposal prep time by 40% and virtually eliminates non-compliant submissions. Teams report catching 3x more implied requirements, significantly improving technical scores.

RACI Matrix Creation (RMC) 95.0%

Measures effectiveness in generating responsibility assignment matrices for complex projects

RMC evaluates an AI's ability to analyze project requirements, team structures, and contractual obligations to automatically generate RACI (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) matrices that clearly define roles and responsibilities throughout the contract lifecycle.

Testing Methodology

  • Role Identification: Extracting all stakeholder roles from RFP/PWS
  • Task Decomposition: Breaking down SOW into assignable activities
  • Accountability Mapping: Single accountable party per deliverable
  • Consultation Requirements: Identifying mandatory review cycles
  • Scalability: Handling projects from 10 to 1000+ tasks
Complex Project Test:
$500M DISA Enterprise IT Services contract with 85 CLINs

C: Quality Lead, Customer | I: All Task Leads

Integration with Contract Management

Modern RACI matrices must align with FAR 42.302 COR responsibilities and integrate with contractor performance assessment reporting systems (CPARS). AI-generated matrices now include automated QASP alignment and performance metric assignments.

Business Impact

Automated RACI generation reduces project kickoff time by 60% and prevents 90% of responsibility-related disputes. Teams report clearer accountability chains and 50% faster decision-making on complex programs.
